Ice is less dense than water

WHY?

Hydrogen bonds is the cause.
Water becomes less dense when it turns into a solid.

Example:

In spring, ice melts, so the weight becomes denser and the ice sinks.

The remaining water will be exposed and vulnerable to further freezing, increasing the possibility of the lake freezing solid throughout the winter.

Benefit

  • Floating ice protect the liquid below it.
    This allow marine life to survive under the floating ice sheets.
